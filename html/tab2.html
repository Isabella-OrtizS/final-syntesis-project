=Special relativity=

The theory of special relativity, also known as the assumption of restricted relativity, is a theory of the most important theories in the field of physics, published in 1905 by Albert Einstein- (1879-1955). Which basically states that light travels at 300,000 km/s and that nothing in the world can overcome this speed. In addition, what is space and time are flexible or not absolute magnitudes, their perception is relative to the person. An example, if for person number one, a video lasted 30 minutes, for person number 2 it can last 10 minutes. So physical magnitudes like, space, time, mass, moment and many more depend on the expectation of each observer.

Basically special relativity is used mainly when huge energies, ultra-fast speeds and astronomical distances are manifested, all without the intervention of gravity.



An example could be: In a hypothetical world where the speed of light is only 40 km/h, all means of transport are restricted to not exceeding such speed. If a man needs to arrive for a job interview, he must request a taxi two hours in advance, even if the journey lasts only about 10 minutes. For the taxi driver, the journey lasts 10 minutes, but for the rest of the world, it lasts 2 hours. This is due to the deformation of time at speeds close to that of light.

In this case, the taxi moves at a speed of 35 km/h, which is significantly lower than the speed of light. However, even at this relatively low speed, time warps, causing driver time and rest of the world time to be perceived differently.

This raises interesting questions about the nature of time and speed, and how our perceptions of these concepts change as we approach the speed of light. If the taxi’s speed were closer to that of light, for example, time would warp even more, leading to even more extreme differences in the perception of time.

In conclusion, special relativity describes the relationship between space and time for things moving at constant speed.


= The constancy of the speed of light in the universe =

The speed of light in a vacuum is a universal constant with the value of 299'792.458 m/s, although usually approaching 3 108 m/s. It is symbolized with the letter c ( means: speed )


= What special relativity has to do with time travel? =




In the case of time travel, the theory of special relativity suggests that an object moving at very high speeds in space-time could experience time differently than we experience on Earth. This could open the possibility of traveling into the future, but not into the past. Example: A study NASA did on twins Scott and Mark Kelly. Which was that one of the brothers stayed in space for 340 days (2015 and 2016), while his twin stayed on earth; and the experiment proves that the astronaut aged less than his twin and had some body changes.
